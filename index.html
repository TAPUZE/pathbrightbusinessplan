A Definitive Business Plan

An interactive presentation of the PathBright Learning investment thesis, market analysis, and strategic roadmap.

Executive Summary

PathBright is poised to capture the U.S. high school equivalency market by providing a superior, mobile-first learning platform. Our core advantage is an exclusive partnership providing a scientifically-validated personalization engine, enabling us to de-risk development and enter the market with a technologically superior product.

The Market

Addressing the ~450,000 annual GED seekers left underserved by the decline of traditional adult education infrastructure.

The Method

A dynamic learning loop of granular diagnostics, multi-modal delivery, and intelligent remediation to ensure mastery and build learner confidence.

The Ask

Seeking $1.2M in seed funding for an 18-month runway to integrate technology, acquire 10,000 users, and secure 3-5 pilot partnerships.

The Market Opportunity

The market represents a stable, multi-million dollar opportunity defined by a structural shift away from traditional learning models, creating a vacuum for a superior digital solution.

Total Addressable Market (TAM)

>39 million U.S. adults without a high school credential.

Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM)

~450,000 individuals actively preparing for the GED annually.

Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM)

Initial 3-5 year target of 25,000 users, representing a $3M ARR opportunity.

Strategic Tailwind

Employment for adult education teachers is projected to decline 13% by 2033. This institutional contraction shifts demand directly to digital platforms like PathBright.

The PathBright Solution

Our product is an intelligent, adaptive ecosystem. Our foundational strength is an exclusive U.S. licensing agreement with a leading international EdTech research firm, providing a scientifically-validated personalization engine.

1

The Diagnostic Journey

The system analyzes not just answers, but "meta-data"—hesitation times, interaction patterns—to build a deep profile of the user's cognitive processing.

2

Multi-Modal Lessons

Content is delivered in micro-learning "snippets" (video, audio, puzzles, games) reverse-engineered from actual GED exams.

3

Intelligent Remediation

If a weakness is detected, the system generates a custom path: a targeted game, a podcast, or even a collaborative multiplayer experience.

4

Building Cognitive Flexibility

The platform strategically encourages users to engage with content formats outside their initial preference to build cognitive resilience.

Go-to-Market Strategy

Our growth is driven by a virtuous cycle: a strong consumer brand drives institutional adoption, and institutional partnerships lend credibility to the consumer offering.

B2C Acquisition

A freemium model will acquire users via targeted digital marketing on TikTok, Instagram, and Facebook. The free tier offers the diagnostic journey to demonstrate platform power and drive conversions.

B2B Partnerships

We will partner with adult education centers, community colleges, and workforce programs, offering a turnkey digital solution via risk-free pilot programs to prove value and secure adoption.

Financials & Vision

We are seeking $1.2M in seed funding for an 18-month runway to achieve key milestones, de-risk the business, and position PathBright for a successful Series A fundraise.

Long-Term Vision

Phase 1 (Years 0-2): Dominate the GED Market.

Phase 2 (Years 2-4): Expand to adjacent credentials (HiSET, vocational certifications).

Phase 3 (Years 5+): Achieve accreditation to become a full-stack credentialing platform, awarding diplomas directly.
